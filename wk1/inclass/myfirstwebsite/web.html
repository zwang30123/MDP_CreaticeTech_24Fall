January 2025 / Understanding Failure

This failure is the point

Tomato and eggs was too smooth for me. I already know how to cook. That's why the transmission felt "incomplete." There was no struggle to reveal anything deeper.

Fortune cookies = unfamiliar territory (baking, not cooking)

The struggle with "appropriate amount," with vague timing, with failure after failure...

This is what transmission actually feels like.

Not smooth. Not predictable. Full of adjustments, guesses, failures.

What the process gave me:

  • Flow state - completely absorbed despite (because of?) difficulty
  • Breaking perfectionism - most looked terrible, but I kept going
  • Understanding "appropriate amount" - now I get why it can't be written precisely
  • Labor as meditation - repetitive making = healing, not just product
  • Failure as learning - the ugly cookies taught me more than perfect ones would
The imperfect fortune cookies better represent our imperfect communication across distance.

Where I am:

This started as a question about recipe transmission. Now it's about what actually gets transmitted when we talk about food across distance. Not just recipes, but worry, care, control, body surveillance, love.

The fortune cookies, the failures, the eating, the attempts to repair... all of it is trying to understand how care travels, transforms, sometimes hurts, but keeps coming anyway.
